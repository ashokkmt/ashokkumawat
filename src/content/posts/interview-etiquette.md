# 💼 Interview Etiquette

Interview etiquette reflects your professionalism, preparation, and attitude even before your technical skills are evaluated. It plays a crucial role in creating a strong first impression and setting the tone for the entire interaction.


## 🔹 Punctuality
Being on time demonstrates discipline, reliability, and respect for the interviewer’s schedule.

- Arrive or log in **10–15 minutes early**
- For online interviews, check your internet, camera, and microphone beforehand
- Avoid last-minute delays or excuses  

> Being early shows enthusiasm; being late creates a negative impression immediately.


## 🔹 Research
Understanding the company and the role shows genuine interest and preparation.

### What to Research:
- Company background, products, and services  
- Recent achievements or news  
- Job description and required skills  
- Company culture and values  

### Why It Matters:
- Helps you tailor your answers  
- Allows you to ask meaningful questions  
- Shows that you are serious about the opportunity  


## 🔹 Dress Code
Your appearance should reflect professionalism and suitability for the role.

### Guidelines:
- Wear **clean, formal, and well-fitted attire**
- Avoid overly flashy or casual clothing  
- Maintain proper grooming and hygiene  

> Dressing well boosts your confidence and leaves a lasting impression.


## 🔹 Professionalism
Professional behavior is reflected in how you communicate and present yourself.

### Key Aspects:
- Greet the interviewer with a polite smile  
- Use formal and respectful language  
- Listen carefully before answering  
- Avoid interrupting  
- Thank the interviewer at the end  


## 🔹 Communication Etiquette
- Speak clearly and confidently  
- Avoid slang or overly casual language  
- Keep your answers structured and relevant  
- Maintain a positive tone throughout  


## 🔹 Do’s and Don’ts

### ✅ Do:
- Maintain eye contact  
- Be honest and authentic  
- Stay calm under pressure  

### ❌ Don’t:
- Speak negatively about past experiences  
- Use filler words excessively  
- Appear distracted or disinterested  


## 🎯 Final Tip
Interview etiquette is not just about rules—it reflects your mindset, respect, and readiness to be part of a professional environment.
